ABOUT                          RESEARCH                       TEACHING                       MISC

 

Much of my research so far has been concerned with problems of population ethics. My BPhil thesis, supervised by Jeff McMahan, consisted of three independent papers. In the first two, I critique Derek Parfit's last attempts to avoid the repugnant conclusion by appealing to the Wide Dual Person-Affecting Principle and the Imprecise Lexical View. In the third one, I offer a deontological solution to the non-identity problem in circumstances of uncertainty.

I retain strong interest in practical ethics. I researched the ethics of emerging technologies under the supervision of Anders Sandberg from the Future of Humanity Institute. In particular, I have written about the moral status of intelligent machines, automation of work, predictive algorithms in the criminal justice system, cognitive enhancement, and existential risk.

In epistemology, I am mostly interested in knowledge-how, epistemic luck, risk, suspended judgment, doubt.
