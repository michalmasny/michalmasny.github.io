Overview

Teaching and mentorship are an important part of my academic life. At Princeton, I have served as a teaching assistant for introductory courses in moral philosophy and designed a novel upper-level seminar on the ethics of emerging technologies, which I co-taught with Johann Frick.

In 2021, I won the Graduate School Teaching Award for "a significant and exceptional contribution to undergraduate teaching".

Technology in the classroom

I'm an advocate for using technology to meet the students' needs in the classroom. In the fall of 2020, I created a video recording studio on campus, and produced and edited Johann Frick's lectures for his "Introduction to Moral Philosophy". The lectures were well-received by the nearly 400 students who took the course, many of which commented on the production value in their evaluations. For example:

"The overall quality of the lectures was great. The content was very interesting and informative, and Professor Frick never let us down with his enthusiasm and insight.
